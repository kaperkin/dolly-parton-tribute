Dolly Parton

"Every single diet I ever fell off of was because of potatoes and gravy of some sort." -Dolly Parton

Dolly Parton's career has spanned nearly five decades and includes singing, song-writing, acting, producing, a theme park, and a charitable organization.

Here is a timeline of Dolly's life

  • 1946 Born on January 19, 1946 in Sevierville, Tennessee in the Great Smoky Mountains
  • 1956 Dolly performs at age 10 for the first time on the Cas Walker show
  • 1959 Dolly sings at the Grand Ole Opry for the first time. She is introduced by Johnny Cash
  • 1964 Dolly moves to Nashville, Tennessee the day after graduating high school to begin her career
  • 1966 Dolly appears on the Porter Wagoner Show for the first time. Also, Dolly marries Carl Thomas Dean on May 30, 1966. They celebrated their 45th anniversary in 2011
  • 1967 Earns her first charting singles, 'Dumb Blonde' at #24, and 'Something Fishy' at #17
  • 1969 Joins the Grand Ole Opry at age 23
  • 1973 'Jolene' hits #1 on the chart; Dolly writes 'I will always love you'
  • 1975 Wins CMA Female Vocalist of the Year
  • 1976 Dolly Syndicated Show airs for the first time
  • 1977 Dolly's album, 'Here You Come Again' sells 1 million copies
  • 1978 Wins CMA Entertainer of the Year
  • 1980 Song '9 to 5' released, reaches #1 on pop and country charts. Wins Grammys for Best Country Vocal Performance by a Female, and Best Country Song. The movie '9 to 5' stars Dolly Parton alongside Jane Fonda and Lilly Tomlin
  • 1982 Stars in 'Best Little Whorehouse in Texas' with Burt Reynolds, featuring her song 'I Will Always Love You'
  • 1986 Dollywood theme park in Pigeon Forge, Tennessee opens
  • 1987 'Dolly' TV Show premieres on ABC
  • 1989 Apears in 'Steel Magnolias'
  • 1991 Dolly becomes Billboard's All-Time Top Female Country Artist
  • 1996 Starts 'Imagination Library' to give back to her community and to promote literacy to the world
  • 1999 Inducted into the Country Music Hall of Fame
  • 2006 Publishes cookbook, Dolly's Dixie Fixin's: Love, Laughter and Lots of Good Food
  • 2015 Dolly Parton's Coat of Many Colors, a made-for-TV film based on Parton's song of the same name airs
